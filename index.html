The truth behind this foundation

We've seen it with our own eyes.

We are Arnav Chouksey and Yashesh Shanishchara — 8th graders from Apex, NC, competing at Core Cricket Academy.

We've had the privilege of real coaching, real facilities. But we've watched friends — equally talented, equally passionate — show up to crumbling, uneven grounds and try to develop the same skills on surfaces that weren't built for cricket.

The ball bounces wrong. The footing is dangerous. Bad habits form. And quietly, slowly, those kids stop coming.

In India, in the Caribbean — countries that produce the world's best players — young athletes get professional surfaces from day one. That's not luck. That's infrastructure. That's investment.

North Carolina has the talent. It doesn't have the fields. Not yet. We're changing that.

Why this works

The field isn't just a field.
It's their whole future.

01
⚡

Technique is built on the surface

A proper pitch gives players consistent, predictable ball behavior. Every hour of practice on quality turf builds muscle memory that transfers directly to competition. Without it, bad habits form — and they're almost impossible to break.

02
🏆

Infrastructure creates champions

India and the Caribbean don't produce world-class cricketers by accident. They build fields, maintain them, and invest in youth infrastructure. North Carolina has the same raw talent — it's waiting for the same investment. That investment starts here.

03
🌱

Communities grow around great facilities

A professional cricket field becomes a hub — tournaments, coaching clinics, school programs, community events. One field investment serves hundreds of athletes for decades. The ripple effect is enormous and permanent.
