Who we are

Built by cricketers,
for cricketers

Youth Wicket Foundation was founded by Arnav Chouksey and Yashesh Shanishchara — two competitive cricketers currently playing for Core Cricket Academy, one of North Carolina's leading academies.

As players who have trained at top-tier facilities, we've witnessed a critical gap firsthand: despite the enormous talent and passion in Apex and Cary, young cricketers are forced to practice on small, neglected grounds that receive little investment or attention.

These grounds pale against the world-class facilities in the Caribbean, India, and Sri Lanka — places where young cricketers develop on meticulously maintained fields from day one. That disparity is holding back an entire generation of North Carolina players.

We realized that this was a problem we could actually solve. So we did something about it.

Our purpose
"Young cricketers in North Carolina deserve the same world-class facilities that their peers in India, the Caribbean, and Sri Lanka train on every single day."

Youth Wicket Foundation provides resources to communities across North Carolina — starting with Apex and Cary — to build professional-grade cricket fields and support youth cricket development at every level of the game.

Why it matters

Why proper fields
change everything

⚡

Proper Technique

Consistent bounce and roll allow players to develop authentic batting and bowling techniques that transfer directly into competitive play at every level.

📈

Faster Development

Young players on quality surfaces progress measurably faster and develop the specific skills needed to compete at regional and national tournaments.

🌟

Community Pride

Professional fields transform communities — establishing Apex and Cary as recognized hubs for youth cricket development throughout North Carolina.

⚖️

The reality: Young cricketers in North Carolina compete against players from India, Pakistan, and the Caribbean who train on world-class facilities every day. Without proper fields, our talent operates at a structural disadvantage. We are leveling the playing field.

The problem we're solving

The challenges facing
our youth

01

Limited Dedicated Facilities

Young cricketers across North Carolina lack dedicated cricket fields. Many train on shared or improvised grounds that simply cannot support proper skill development.

02

Neglected Local Grounds

Existing grounds in Apex and Cary receive minimal maintenance and investment — creating inconsistent surfaces that hinder skill development and pose injury risks.

03

Financial Barriers to Infrastructure

Building professional cricket fields costs $10,000–$25,000+. Communities recognize the value but need philanthropic support to make these critical investments happen.

What we do

Our three-pillar
approach

1

Field Development

We work directly with Apex, Cary, and other North Carolina communities to fund and build professional-grade cricket fields from the ground up, using the highest construction standards.

2

Equipment & Resources

We provide quality training equipment — batting mats, bowling machines, protective gear, and professional training materials — for youth cricket programs throughout the region.

3

Community Partnerships

We partner with local organizations, schools, and academies to create lasting cricket infrastructure that serves communities and develops young talent for decades to come.
